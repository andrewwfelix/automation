The Story in Brief

Catch Me If You Can is the real-life memoir of Frank Abagnale, a brilliant young con man who successfully impersonated an airline pilot, doctor, and lawyer, among other professions, and cashed millions in fraudulent checks across the United States in the 1960s. The story is a thrilling cat-and-mouse game between Abagnale and the tenacious FBI agent Carl Hanratty who pursues him. Abagnale's incredible exploits and the slick, stylish adaptation from Steven Spielberg make this a captivating true crime tale.

Key Differences

Narrative Structure

The book follows a chronological structure, tracing Abagnale's con artist activities in a straightforward manner. The film, however, employs a more playful, non-linear approach, jumping between Abagnale's escapades and Hanratty's investigation. This allows for more dynamic storytelling and character development, with the movie shifting perspectives between the two protagonists.

Tone and Style

While the book maintains a matter-of-fact, journalistic tone, the movie version has a distinctly glossy, almost glamorous aesthetic. Director Steven Spielberg infuses the film with a slick, stylized visual flair, accentuated by the lush cinematography, jazzy score, and vibrant period production design. This lends the adaptation a more playful, cinematic quality compared to the book's grounded realism.

Character Portrayals

The book provides an in-depth, first-person account of Abagnale's mindset and motivations, delving deeper into his psyche. The movie, meanwhile, focuses more on the cat-and-mouse dynamic between Abagnale and Hanratty, with Leonardo DiCaprio and Tom Hanks' performances driving the on-screen chemistry. The film also expands the roles of Abagnale's parents, adding emotional weight to his story.

Thematic Emphasis

While both versions explore themes of identity, the pursuit of the American Dream, and the complex relationship between Abagnale and Hanratty, the film places greater emphasis on the latter. Spielberg's adaptation delves more into the bond that forms between the two men, reflecting on the nature of morality and the fine line between criminal and hero.

Ending Divergence

The book's conclusion is more abrupt, focused on Abagnale's eventual capture and imprisonment. The movie, on the other hand, provides a more bittersweet, reflective ending, following Abagnale's life after his release and the continued relationship between him and Hanratty.

Should You Read First?

Both the book and the movie version of Catch Me If You Can are highly engaging, and the decision of which to experience first largely comes down to personal preference. The book offers a more immersive, first-hand account of Abagnale's exploits, while the film provides a visually stunning, character-driven adaptation. Ultimately, the choice is yours, but either way, you're in for a thrilling ride.

Verdict

A thrilling true story that translates remarkably well to the big screen, making for a close call between the book and the movie.
